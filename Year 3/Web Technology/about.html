About GIAF

Every July, the multidisciplinary Galway International Arts Festivals take place in Galway, Ireland, and cover a wide range of arts forms.

The Galway International Arts Festival (GIAF) is one of the world's leading contemporary arts festivals. The Arts festival programme features Irish and international work of the highest quality, featuring theatre, music, visual arts, street spectacle, dance and comedy.

In addition to the celebrated annual festival, GIAF hosts a First Thought Talks, to present a major discussion platform on crativity and inniovation.
In 2022, over 600 performances took place in 32 venues over the festival of 14 days, with an attendance iin escess of 300,000. The Guardian named our festival as one of the Top 5 Summer Festivals in Europe. To keep in touch; follow Galway International Arts Festival on Facebook, Twitter, Instagra, Youtube or contact us to learning more about GIAF.
Galway International Arts Festival's discussion platform First Thought Talks is a forum for discussion on creativity across a range of fields and disciplines. The programme has developed significantly since its introduction in 2012, featuring many high-profile events and panellists, including President of Ireland Michael D. Higgins, former president Mary Robinson, producer and musician Nile Rodgers and actors John Mahoney and Cillian Murphy. Discussing themes of power, borders and more.
