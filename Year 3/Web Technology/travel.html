Welcome to

Galway

Gateway to the Wild Atlantic Way

Galway marks the half point on the Wild Atlantic Way and is the only city on the entire 2500km route. As well as being a popular seaside destination with long sandy beaches, Galway is also a thriving and cosmopolitan city centre.

Galway is also well known for its proliferation of festivals and civic events, with huge crowd gathering for the much-celebrated Galway International Art Festival, the craic of the Galway Races and numerous other events throughout the year. Ireland's great traditions of hospitality have their place in the city too, with turf fires and traditional music featuring in many of the city's pub.

Transport

Getting around Galway city by bus is relatively straightfoward and due to the extensive inner-city bus network, passengers can get to many different areas aroound the city easily.

The bus routes usually start at Eyre Square (city centre) and travel outwards to one or more suburbs.
